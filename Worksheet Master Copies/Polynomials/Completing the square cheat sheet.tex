\Large

A step-by-step guide to solving quadratic equations by completing the square, with a running example of $-7x^2+7x+42=0$:

\vspace{5mm}

\begin{colenum}
\additem{\textbf{Step-by-step instructions:}}{\textbf{Running example:} $-7x^2+7x+42=0$}
\additem{If the coefficient on the $x^2$-term is not 1, divide through by this coefficient (including the sign)}{$x^2-x-6=0$}
\additem{Take $a$ to be half of the $x$-coefficient (including the sign)}{Take $a=-\frac{1}{2}$}
\additem{Write out the expansion of $(x+a)^2=x^2+2ax+a^2$ with your particular value of $a$}{$\left(x-\frac{1}{2}\right)^2=x^2-x+\frac{1}{4}$}
\additem{Subtract $a^2$ to be left with the first two terms of the original equation}{$\left(x-\frac{1}{2}\right)^2-\frac{1}{4}=x^2-x$}
\additem{Add the constant term from the original equation, so that your new expression is equal to the original one}{$\left(x-\frac{1}{2}\right)^2-\frac{1}{4}-6=x^2-x-6=0$}
\additem{Move the constant terms across to the other side of the equation and simplify}{$\left(x-\frac{1}{2}\right)^2=6+\frac{1}{4}=\frac{24}{4}+\frac{1}{4}=\frac{25}{4}$}
\additem{Square root both sides, remembering plus or minus}{$x-\frac{1}{2}=\pm\sqrt{\frac{25}{4}}=\frac{\pm\sqrt{25}}{\sqrt{4}}=\frac{\pm5}{2}$}
\additem{Move the remaining constant term to the other side of the equation to leave $x$ isolated on the left}{$x=\frac{1}{2}\pm\frac{5}{2}$}
\additem{Evaluate the two possibilities of the plus or minus to get the roots}{$x=\frac{6}{2} \mbox{ or } \frac{-4}{2}=3\mbox{ or } -2$}
\end{colenum}
